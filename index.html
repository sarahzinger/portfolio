Hi there. I'm Sarah.

I love to create things, build communities, and solve problems

I've worked as a social worker, community organizer, and most recently as Executive Director of a new tech nonprofit called Americans Give. I am fascinated by the use of technology for social good and am an aspiring web developer.

I studied Sociology and Women's Studies at Vassar College, where I worked at the Women's Center and first took a computer science class. While at Vassar, I found I was passionate about student activism and went on to get my Master in Social Work with a focus on Community Organizing, Planning and Development at Hunter College. In my studies, I interned with NAMI NYC Metro and Hudson Link.

After graduating, I worked for two years as a social worker in Brooklyn and Queens. Through this work I learned invaluable lessons about myself and the world around me. I left with a conviction to find innovative ways to improve access to vital funding and technology for nonprofits, which led me to my current role as Executive Director of Americans Give, a new small tech-focused nonprofit, which seeks to reinvision how Americans donate to campaigns and causes.

In my free time I love to cook, write, and code. For a copy of my resume feel free to email me here. For a glimpse at some of my recent projects, check out my portfolio below.
